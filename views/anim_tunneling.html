Quantum Tunneling

About this phenomenon

In classical mechanics, a particle cannot pass through a potential barrier if its energy is lower than the barrier's height. However, in quantum mechanics, particles have a wavelike nature. This means there is a non-zero probability that the particle can "tunnel" through the barrier and appear on the other side, a phenomenon crucial to functions like nuclear fusion in stars and the operation of modern semiconductors.
